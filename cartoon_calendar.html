Certifiably Silly · Calendar History · Animated Governance

What If Cartoons
Named the Months?

They convened. They argued. They voted. The results were chaotic, deeply personal, and — in the opinion of several attendees — better than what we currently have.

Official Proceedings · The Cartoon Calendar Convention · Est. 1953 (According to Bugs Bunny, Who Forged the Documents)

In the spring of 1953 — which, for reasons that will shortly become apparent, would have been renamed "Zoinksuary" — the animated characters of the world convened for the First and Final Cartoon Calendar Convention, a summit called to address what Bugs Bunny described as "the frankly embarrassing state of monthly nomenclature." The proceedings were chaotic, often loud, and occasionally on fire. They produced twelve proposed month names, none of which were adopted. The Romans kept their calendar. The cartoons kept their grievances. This is that story.

The Convention had been proposed by Bugs Bunny himself, who had long maintained that the existing month names were, in his estimation, "stuffy, Latinate, and disappointingly low on gags." He had circulated a memo — forged on official-looking stationery he produced from behind his back — to every major animated character of the era. Attendance was technically optional. In practice, no one wanted to explain to Bugs why they hadn't shown up.

The meeting was held in a cartoon cave somewhere in Arizona, which was painted to look like a tunnel and turned out to be a real tunnel, which caused no fewer than four characters to run through the wall and into a freight train. After some delay, proceedings began.

Elmer Fudd moved to rename all twelve months "Hunting Season." The motion was tabled, though only after a lengthy parliamentary argument about whether Elmer was allowed to vote since he, technically, never wins anything.

The Official Record · Month by MonthThe Twelve Proposed Names

What follows is the complete record of the Convention's proposed names for the twelve months, along with the character who proposed each name, the reasoning provided (such as it was), and any objections raised. The record was transcribed by Tweety Bird, who is reliably accurate but writes very small.

01
January
Colduary
🥶
Proposed by: Bugs Bunny

"January," Bugs argued, "tells you nothing. Colduary tells you everything you need to know about this miserable month, and I for one am tired of being surprised by it." He noted that the name should be pronounced with a long "Cold" and a suffering sigh after "uary."

Objection: Elmer Fudd moved to rename it "Hunting Season, Part One." Motion lost 47–1. Elmer's vote was also lost, as he had accidentally written it on his own forehead.
02
February
Blehuary
😑
Proposed by: Eeyore

Eeyore's proposal was, at fourteen seconds, the shortest presentation of the Convention. "February," he said, "is the month where everything is still terrible but winter hasn't ended yet and Valentine's Day makes you feel worse than you already did, if that's possible, which for me it always is. I propose Blehuary. It ends in 'uary' because that is what the Calendar Committee apparently wants, and things rarely go the way I'd prefer, so." He then sat down. The room was quiet. Several characters cried.

Objection: Winnie the Pooh suggested "Hunny-uary" as an amendment. Eeyore stared at him for a long time and then looked at the floor.
03
March
Sproinger
🌱
Proposed by: Tigger

Tigger maintained that the third month of the year, when things begin waking up and bouncing back to life, deserved a name that properly conveyed the sensation. "March," he said, "sounds like something you do reluctantly to school. Sproinger sounds like what the whole world does when it realizes winter is basically over, which is the WONDERFUL thing about it!" He noted approvingly that the "-er" ending gave it a certain "bounceback quality." He then bounced three times during the proposal. The podium did not survive.

Objection: Eeyore noted that Sproinger "still sounds unnecessarily cheerful for a month that frequently snows on you without warning."
04
April
Sufferuary
🌦️
Proposed by: Sylvester the Cat

Sylvester argued that April's famous weather inconsistency — warm one day, cold the next, raining sideways, then somehow sunny at 4pm — deserved a name that expressed the appropriate emotional response. "Sufferuary," he maintained, was both accurate and elegant. "It retains the dignity of a proper month name while making absolutely clear what it does to you," he said. He had originally proposed "Sufferin' Succotash" but withdrew it after Tweety Bird pointed out the initials. "Sufferuary thuffers no thuch problem," Sylvester declared.

Objection: Tweety Bird noted that Sylvester had pronounced "suffers" and "such" incorrectly during his own correction. Sylvester chased Tweety out of the room. The vote was delayed 45 minutes.
05
May
Zoinksuary
😱
Proposed by: Shaggy Rogers

Shaggy, who arrived to the Convention late and appeared to have been running from something, explained that May deserved a name capturing the sudden realization that summer is approaching and nothing is prepared. "Like, every May I look up and go 'Zoinks, it's already May,'" he testified. "Every single year. I originally said just Zoinks, but someone on the committee said it needed a proper ending, so — Zoinksuary. It's all the same feeling but now it sounds official." Scooby-Doo endorsed the proposal with a thumbs up.

Objection: Fred Jones said the name lacked professionalism. Shaggy noted that Fred's ascot also lacked professionalism. The resulting silence lasted several minutes.
06
June
D'oh
🌞
Proposed by: Homer Simpson

Homer arrived via unauthorized time portal and immediately proposed renaming June "D'oh," explaining that it was the month when school ended, which was great, but also when he was expected to plan things with the family, which was not. "D'oh is not a name for a bad month," he clarified. "D'oh is a name for a complicated month. A month with mixed feelings. A month you didn't think through." He was briefly challenged on the grounds that all other proposed names ended in 'ary' or 'er,' at which point Homer said, "D'oh," which everyone agreed was an entirely appropriate response to being told that. He then ate four donuts during the discussion period.

Objection: Several old-guard characters objected to a "future cartoon" participating. Homer told them not to think of it as the future, just a different Tuesday.
07
July
Blastember
🎆
Proposed by: Yosemite Sam

Yosemite Sam presented his proposal in person, and then also presented it again via three gunshots fired at the ceiling, which he maintained were "part of the pitch." July, he argued, was the month of the Fourth of July, and the Fourth of July was fireworks, and fireworks were essentially the universe confirming that his approach to communication was correct all along. His original name had been "BANG BANG BANG," but the Committee had informed him it needed to end in 'er.' "FINE," he roared. "BLASTEMBER. It blasts AND it ends in 'er.' ARE YOU HAPPY?" He fired twice more. The Committee marked it as approved.

Objection: Daffy Duck noted that "Blastember" sounded like it should be September. Sam shot Daffy's hat off. Daffy noted this did not address the calendar issue.
08
August
Sweater
🥵
Proposed by: Daffy Duck

Daffy, who was still adjusting his hat, proposed "Sweater" on the grounds that August was the hottest, most miserable, most aggressively humid month on the calendar and that the name should reflect this. "You are wearing a thweater," he said, "whether you packed one or not. Becauthe it ith on you. Ath moischtüre." He had originally said "Sweatember" but was told by the Committee that the name needed to end in 'er.' Daffy stared at them and said the existing proposal, correctly formatted, was "Sweater," and that this was also funnier, which he stood by entirely.

Objection: Bugs pointed out that "Sweater" was already a word for a garment. Daffy replied that August was already a word for a person of distinguished rank, and nobody seemed bothered by that.
09
September
Mehtember
🍂
Proposed by: SpongeBob SquarePants

SpongeBob, who also arrived via unauthorized time portal and apologized warmly for several minutes before getting to the point, proposed "Mehtember" for September on the basis that it was neither summer nor fall, neither exciting nor terrible — it was simply the month where everything was fine, which SpongeBob found somehow the most unsettling quality a month could possess. He had been told to ensure the name ended in 'er,' and he pointed out that Mehtember already did, having apparently planned ahead, which was unusual for September. "I'm ready!" he said. "But for what? That's Mehtember."

Objection: Patrick Star, who had followed SpongeBob through the portal, asked if they could name a month "Patrickember" instead. SpongeBob said that wasn't on the agenda. Patrick said he was still proud of the idea.
10
October
Spookember
👻
Proposed by: Scooby-Doo

Scooby-Doo presented his proposal for October personally, noting that it was the month of Halloween, mysteries, haunted houses, and creatures that turned out to be real estate developers in masks, and that this was essentially his whole biography. He had initially proposed "Scooby-Dooby-Doo," but upon being informed the name needed to end in 'er,' he conferred briefly with Shaggy and returned with "Spookember." "Rooky!" he said, which Shaggy translated as "Spooky" and "October" combined, and also probably perfect. Several delegates agreed this was the most logical reasoning heard all day, which said something about the overall quality of the Convention.

Objection: Shaggy noted that "Spookember" sounded like it should be September. Scooby stared at him. Shaggy immediately withdrew the objection.
11
November
Gobbluary
🦃
Proposed by: Bart Simpson

Bart arrived shortly after Homer, and immediately proposed renaming November "Gobbluary" — a name that honored Thanksgiving, sounded vaguely official, and was, in his professional assessment, "still pretty rad." He noted that it ended in 'uary' because the Committee kept going on about that, and 'gobble' because that was what turkeys said, what people did all month, and also what happened to his homework on a regular basis. He gave himself a thumbs up.

Objection: Homer objected on grounds that the name contained no reference to donuts. Bart reminded him they were naming a month. Homer asked if the month would come with food. The question was not resolved.
12
December
Holycowember
🎄
Proposed by: Mickey Mouse

Mickey Mouse, who had been quietly waiting for his turn since the Convention opened and had twice been accidentally sat on by Dumbo, finally rose to propose "Holycowember" for December. He had originally said "Ho Ho Holy Cow," which he argued captured the entire emotional arc of the month: the initial Christmas joy ("Ho Ho"), the dawning realization of how much there is to do ("Holy Cow"), and the eventual warm exhaustion of year's end. When the Committee informed him the name needed to end in 'er,' Mickey cheerfully merged the whole phrase into "Holycowember," which he felt captured all the same feelings and also sounded legitimately like a month. The room gave him a standing ovation. Even Eeyore clapped, slowly, in a minor key.

Objection: Goofy noted that "Holy Cow" was also something he said, and asked if he could get partial credit for the month. Mickey, graciously, said yes.

The Vote · Official TallyWhy None of This Was Adopted

The vote on the final calendar took place at 4:47 pm, at which point the cave had been partially collapsed by Road Runner and rebuilt by Wile E. Coyote (using an Acme kit that worked perfectly on the first attempt, which everyone agreed was suspicious). The final vote on whether to submit the proposed calendar to human governments was as follows:

The calendar passed 7–0 (with one abstention and one ballot invalidated for being exclusively about hunting). It was then submitted to the International Calendar Authority in Geneva, Switzerland, which replied three weeks later with a letter that said, politely but firmly, that they were not accepting calendars from anyone, animated or otherwise, and suggested the signatories return to their regularly scheduled programming.

The Dissenters: Characters Who Refused to Attend

Wile E. Coyote sent his regrets, explaining that he had ordered an Acme Calendar Convention Kit and was waiting for delivery. The kit arrived the day after the Convention, parachuted in from great height, and landed on him.

Tom and Jerry RSVP'd yes but arrived fighting and had to be separated by the entire cave, which collapsed, which turned out to be Wile E. Coyote's fault after all.

The Road Runner arrived, said "Meep meep," and left. Nobody is sure if that was a vote.

Popeye submitted a written proposal to rename all twelve months after spinach. It was a single page that said "SPINACH" twelve times, each in slightly larger letters. He was thanked for his submission.

The Convention was formally closed at 6:00 pm by Bugs Bunny, who wrapped up proceedings by looking directly at the audience and saying, "And that's all, folks." This was considered a technically correct statement but also felt a little on the nose, even for a cartoon.

The twelve proposed names remain, to this day, unofficial. But every Colduary, when the cold arrives unremarked upon, and every Blehuary, when things are simply bleh, and every Holycowember when you look at your to-do list and feel the whole arc of the year collapsing onto you — the spirit of the Convention lives on. It was, in the end, exactly right. It just wasn't official.

The Romans, for their part, have never apologized.

The next Convention is scheduled for whenever Bugs Bunny feels like it.
