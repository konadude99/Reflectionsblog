Cars are fully aware they are the responsible party in the relationship.

They know everything.

Your habits. Your mistakes. Your weird fast-food parking-lot rituals. And exactly how long you ignore dashboard warning lights before panic-Googling them.

Filed Grievances
Complaint #001 · Highway Division

"You Know I Can't Teleport, Right?"

— Your Car During Merging

You wait until the final possible nanosecond to merge onto the interstate. Every day. You approach highway traffic at the speed of an anxious turtle while muttering "come on… come on…"

Meanwhile the car is thinking: "I have given you 280 horsepower and modern engineering. Why are we entering traffic like a hesitant canoe?"

280 HP Ignored Canoe Energy
→ The gap was right there. It is now 4 miles behind you.
Complaint #002 · Suspension Dept.

"Ah Yes. Another Pothole."

— Your Suspension System

Cars remember every pothole. Every single one. You may forget the crater you hit outside the grocery store in February. Your suspension does not.

It remembers. It discusses it with the tires at night.

Your car especially resents that you clearly saw the pothole, had ample room to avoid it, and hit it anyway — with the precision of a fighter pilot.

Zero Excuse Perfect Aim Fighter Pilot Energy
→ "Perhaps next we drive directly into a mailbox."
Complaint #003 · Fuel Tank Office

"The Gas Light Is Not a Fun Challenge."

— Your Fuel Tank

Some drivers refill at half a tank. Others treat the low-fuel warning like a motivational suggestion.

Your car watches in horror as you continue driving 12 miles to work, 9 miles to Target, 4 miles for tacos, and somehow another 17 miles "just to see."

Fumes Prayer Tacos Worth It?
→ The fuel gauge is no longer informational. It's emotional.
Complaint #004 · Tire Division

"We Are Not in NASCAR."

— Your Tires During Rain

Rain begins. Visibility disappears. Roads resemble reflective soup. And somehow you decide: "This seems like a good time to go 84."

Meanwhile your tires are hydroplaning toward existential reflection.

Wet Roads 84 mph Tires Weeping
→ The tires did not consent to this.
Complaint #005 · Engine Collective

"Oh, Now You Hear That Sound."

— The Entire Engine

For months your car has been producing grinding, squealing, knocking, wheezing — or a noise best described as "angry dishwasher full of bolts."

You ignored all of it. Then one morning: "Huh. That doesn't sound good."

The car is stunned by this breakthrough.

Months Ignored Dishwasher Bolts Breakthrough
→ The mechanic already knew. The car already knew. You were last.
Complaint #006 · Neighboring Vehicles

"You Absolutely Could Have Parked Better."

— Nearby Vehicles

Your car knows when you park badly. It knows when you straighten three times, give up, and leave the vehicle diagonally like a fleeing bank robber.

Others pull six inches from another vehicle, over the line, or directly onto the curb with the confidence of medieval siege equipment.

Diagonal Three Attempts Medieval Confidence
→ Compact cars everywhere whisper: "He thinks he nailed it."
Complaint #007 · Interior Upholstery

"Why Are We Here Again?"

— Your Car in the Drive-Thru

Cars know your order patterns. They know the "rough week" milkshake stops, the secret taco runs, and the suspicious number of French fries discovered beneath the seats.

Your car's interior has become an archaeological dig of receipts, crumbs, mystery napkins, and one fossilized french fry from 2019.

Crumbs Mystery Napkins 2019 Fry
→ The upholstery has filed a formal complaint with the seats.
Complaint #008 · Society at Large

"The Turn Signal Is Free."

— Society

The technology is right there. One finger. A tiny click. Instead, some drivers change lanes using only mystery and aggression.

Other cars interpret these maneuvers the way gazelles interpret nearby lions.

One Finger Free of Charge Gazelle Protocol
→ This is not a mystery novel. Signal.
Complaint #009 · Brake Pad Association

"You Don't Need to Brake for Every Emotional Event."

— Your Brake Pads

Some people drive with smooth precision. Others brake like they just saw a ghost cross the highway.

Your car especially fears drivers who accelerate hard, immediately brake, then repeat this pattern every 40 feet.

Ghost Detected 40 Feet Astronaut Passengers
→ Passengers are subtly bracing like astronauts during reentry.
Complaint #010 · Paint Job Dept.

"I Saw What Happened at the Car Wash."

— Your Paint Job

Your car enters thinking: "Perhaps today is maintenance day." Then suddenly giant spinning noodles attack, water cannons fire from all directions, and soap slaps the windshield with biblical intensity.

The entire experience feels like an industrial squid encounter.

Spinning Noodles Biblical Soap Industrial Squid
→ Your car emerged cleaner and more traumatized.
Complaint #011 · Interior Cabin

"You Talk to Yourself A Lot."

— The Interior Cabin

Cars hear everything. Everything.

Your car knows your road rage vocabulary, your off-key singing, your fake arguments replayed aloud, and your deeply ambitious plans made while driving at night.

The steering wheel has heard TED Talks, therapy sessions, and karaoke disasters.

Road Rage Vocab Karaoke Disaster Ambitious Plans
→ Your car knows your true self. And frankly, it's concerned.
Complaint #012 · The Guy Behind You

"This Isn't a Race."

— The Guy Behind You at the Stoplight

The light turns green. The car ahead accelerates normally. But one driver launches forward with such violence that nearby coffee achieves orbit.

For what purpose? Everyone meets at the next red light anyway.

Coffee: Orbital Next Light: Same
→ Your car is embarrassed on your behalf.
Complaint #013 · Shock Absorber Dept.

"Oh Good. A Speed Bump at 37 MPH."

— Your Shock Absorbers

Speed bumps are not merely decorative. Some drivers approach cautiously. Others attack speed bumps like military maneuvers, physics experiments, or revenge missions against their own suspension.

Your car survives these encounters the way shopping carts survive tornadoes.

37 MPH Revenge Mission Shopping Cart Mode
→ The suspension has started keeping a journal.

Breaking: Interior Cabin Issues Statement

"We have recorded your road rage vocabulary, your off-key singing, your fake arguments rehearsed at 65 mph, and your deeply ambitious 11pm life plans that are, frankly, never going to happen. The steering wheel has heard things. It cannot unhear them. We are all concerned."

And Despite Everything…

Cars spend years quietly observing humanity — our impatience, our snack habits, our inability to merge, and our irrational confidence in parallel parking.

And despite everything, they still start every morning.

Mostly.

Unless they've finally decided: "You know what? Walk."
