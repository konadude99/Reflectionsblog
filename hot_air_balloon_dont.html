🎈 A Safety Briefing Nobody Asked For
🎈

15 Things You Should NEVER Do
on a Hot Air Balloon Ride

A strictly scientific, entirely authoritative, and absolutely necessary guide to not ruining what should be a perfectly lovely morning.

🔥 Open Flame Nearby ☁️ Altitude: High 🧺 Wicker Basket 😬 Regret: Possible

Every year, thousands of people float serenely above the earth in hot air balloons, watching the world below like gods who woke up early. Most of them are fine. This guide is dedicated to the ones who were not. Or rather, the ones who nearly weren't. Because they did these things.

🎈 The Fifteen Commandments — Balloon Edition
01
🍲
Announce You Left the Stove On
Announce mid-ascent that you "just remembered" you left the stove on. Then spend the entire flight narrating your concerns about an unattended pot of soup while everyone else tries to enjoy the sunrise. Nobody can help you up here, Gerald. The soup is gone.
→ The soup was already gone before you left.
02
💨
Bring a Leaf Blower to Help
The pilot has been doing this for 20 years. The pilot has instruments, a burner, and a deep understanding of thermals. You have a Black & Decker and a can-do attitude. The pilot will not appreciate your initiative. You will not be thanked. Leave the leaf blower.
→ You are not the co-pilot. You are a passenger.
03
🗺️
Ask "Are We There Yet?"
You are floating. There is no "there." You are already everywhere and nowhere simultaneously, drifting on the wind like a dandelion seed with anxiety. The destination is the journey. Please stop asking. The pilot has nowhere to pull over.
→ There is no "there." There is only sky.
04
🐦
Attempt to Pet a Passing Bird
You will not succeed. The bird is traveling on its own itinerary and has not agreed to be petted. You will, however, definitely tip the basket. Everyone else will grab the side. The bird will be completely unbothered. You will not.
→ The bird doesn't know you. The bird doesn't care.
05
🛵
Order DoorDash to Your Current Location
You are in motion. You are airborne. You are covering approximately six miles per hour in a direction determined by the wind. The delivery driver's review will simply read "address kept moving" and one star. The burritos will be placed in a field. A confused cow will eat them.
→ The cow doesn't tip either.
06
🌍
Start the Flat Earth Argument
You are literally looking at the curvature of the Earth right now. It is in front of your face. The horizon is doing its thing. Let it go, Gerald. This is not the venue. You paid three hundred dollars to be here. Look at the beautiful round Earth and say nothing.
→ The Earth remains spherical at all altitudes.
07
☂️
Open an Umbrella "Just in Case"
You will become a Mary Poppins situation. The wind will catch the umbrella. You will become briefly and terrifyingly airborne relative to the already-airborne balloon. Nobody has time for a balloon inside a balloon scenario at 3,000 feet. Leave the umbrella in the car.
→ You are already above the rain. Think about it.
08
🪅
Bring a Piñata
You will swing at it, miss, and the candy will rain down on a farmer's field 2,000 feet below. The farmer will look up, bewildered. The goats will be traumatized. The toffees will be found three counties away. The donkey inside the piñata will haunt the valley.
→ The goats will never forgive you.
09
😰
Reveal Mid-Flight You're Scared of Heights
This information had a much more useful window. That window was on the ground, before you got in. Or even before you paid. Or before you googled "hot air balloon rides near me." It had many, many windows. All of those windows are now closed, and below you.
→ The ground is still there. Just further away.
10
🎈
Flag Down Another Balloon for Directions
You are in the sky. The directions are: stay in the sky, then eventually don't. The other balloon cannot give you a turn-by-turn. They are also floating on the wind. They don't know where they're going either. Everyone up here is simply vibing and pretending otherwise.
→ We are all lost up here. That's the point.
11
🔥
Bring a Barbecue Grill
There is already a giant open flame directly above your head. It is the size of a Buick. It is what is keeping you alive. You do not need more fire. You do not need a portable Weber up here. You need to reflect on your choices and put the charcoal away.
→ One fireball per balloon. This is not negotiable.
12
🏡
Call Your Real Estate Agent
"I've found a neighborhood I like. It's between the clouds and that farm — very open floor plan, great natural light, motivated seller. The cows might be negotiable." They will not be negotiable. The cows do not negotiate. Your agent will hang up. You will be recharged a cancellation fee.
→ The sky is not zoned for residential.
13
🎵
Practice Your Yodeling
You will echo across four counties. You will shatter the peaceful morning silence. A small village below will believe they have angered a mountain god. The mountain god does not live here. You do not yodel. Or perhaps you do now. Either way, stop. Everyone is looking at the basket.
→ The valley has ears. Use yours to stay quiet.
14
🤧
Sneeze on the Flame
Just… don't. We shouldn't have to explain this one. There is a giant propane-fed flame heating the air inside the envelope that is keeping all of you in the sky. Aim away from the flame. Cover your mouth. Bring a handkerchief. This is basic balloon etiquette.
→ We really cannot stress this one enough.
15
😤
Complain About the In-Flight Movie
You were in a wicker basket attached to a balloon. The entire sky was your movie. Every cloud, every field, every tiny car below you was the most cinematic morning of your life. And you wanted a screen. You are impossible to please. Please do not book again.
→ Five-star views. Zero screens. Exactly as intended.

⚠ Official Advisory Note

The management of this balloon (and this article) would like to remind all passengers that hot air ballooning is, statistically, a remarkably safe and beautiful activity. None of the above incidents have caused any recorded fatalities. Several have caused mild embarrassment, one lasting friendship with a confused farmer, and at least two goats who now look up at the sky every morning, waiting.

Now Go Float.
Responsibly.

The sky is up there. The basket is waiting. The pilot has had three coffees and a philosophical outlook on wind patterns. Leave the leaf blower, pack a sense of wonder, and for the love of all that is aloft — don't sneeze on the flame.
