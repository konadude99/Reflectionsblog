Tier II  •  The Academic Class

Granola

"Did I mention I went to grad school?"

ABD (All But Dissertation) Reusable Tote Owner Podcast Host, Unpublished

Granola did not, technically, finish its dissertation. This is a topic you should avoid at brunch, at farmers' markets, and especially in the bulk foods aisle, where Granola will trap you for forty-five minutes explaining why "the academy failed it." The thesis was on Foucauldian power structures in pre-industrial grain harvesting. The committee "just wasn't ready."

Granola has a strong opinion about your coffee, your news source, your yoga mat brand, and the particular species of blueberry you chose to mix with it. All opinions are delivered with the serene confidence of someone who once audited a philosophy seminar and retained nothing except the smug.

Notably, Granola refers to itself as a "craft" cereal and will correct you if you call it "just trail mix." It is not just trail mix. It went to grad school.

Known Associations & Activities
  • Has a Substack about "conscious snacking" (12 subscribers, all from the dissertation cohort)
  • Once argued with a barista about oat milk for 22 consecutive minutes
  • Quotes David Foster Wallace ironically but also sincerely
  • Insists on being called "Artisanal Granola" in formal settings
Tier IV  •  Currently on Parole

Lucky Charms

"The marshmallows are purely decorative. I am not buying."

3 Prior Offenses Charm Trafficking Magically Delicious (disputed)

Lucky Charms has a record. We're not going to say what it is exactly, because Lucky Charms' lawyer (a very small and excitable Leprechaun named Gerald) has sent us a strongly worded cease-and-desist. What we can say is that the marshmallow-to-oat ratio discrepancy of 2019 "just happened," the rainbow was not a getaway device, and the horseshoe was entirely coincidental.

Lucky Charms operates in what law enforcement calls "the charm economy" — a shadow market of shooting stars, blue moons, and red balloons whose street value fluctuates wildly among the under-8 demographic. The FBI has opened a file. The file is shaped like a clover.

Lucky Charms is currently completing 200 hours of community service, which involves appearing in cereal commercials. This is, somehow, part of the plea deal.

Criminal Rap Sheet
  • Count 1: Grand larceny of children's attention spans (2009–present)
  • Count 2: Operating a marshmallow distribution ring without a license
  • Count 3: False advertising — nobody has ever proven it is lucky
  • Currently on parole; must check in with Snap, Crackle, and Pop weekly
Tier I  •  Landed Gentry

Grape-Nuts

"The property is in a trust. Several trusts."

Landlord Contains No Grapes Contains No Nuts Old Money

Grape-Nuts owns property. Not just a little property. Grape-Nuts owns the building that Corn Flakes lives in, three vacation homes in New England, a timeshare it pretends not to have, and a worrying amount of farmland in states it has never visited. Grape-Nuts does not explain how it acquired these assets. This is considered rude to ask.

The name "Grape-Nuts" contains neither grapes nor nuts, which is exactly the kind of mystifying brand opacity that old money families have relied on for generations. Grape-Nuts did not get rich by being understood. It got rich by being confusing and reliable and vaguely associated with health in the 1890s when the bar for "health food" was "contains no active cholera."

Grape-Nuts has strong opinions about estate planning, compound interest, and "the kind of people" who eat cereal with added sugar. It will not elaborate on who those people are. It doesn't have to.

Holdings & Interests
  • Fourteen rental units; raises rent annually "due to market conditions"
  • Founding member of the Pantry Shelf Homeowners Association
  • Has sent 3 strongly worded letters to Froot Loops about the noise
  • Net worth: unknowable, which is exactly how Grape-Nuts likes it

Tier IV  •  Suspected Fraud

Cap'n Crunch

"I am absolutely a captain. Please don't look that up."

Rank Disputed Roof of Mouth Hazard Crunch Berries (Unexplained)

Cap'n Crunch is not a captain. The U.S. Navy has confirmed this. The U.S. Coast Guard has confirmed this. The International Maritime Organization has confirmed this. Cap'n Crunch's response to all confirmation is to simply point to the hat, which does have a lot of gold on it, so people just... go along with it.

Cap'n Crunch is, however, a first-rate grifter. The cereal has spent decades convincing the American public that lacerating the roof of their mouth is simply the price of deliciousness, rather than what it technically is, which is a design failure. Crunch berries have never been explained. They are not a berry. They are not a crunch. They are simply There, and we have all agreed to accept them.

Naval authorities suspect Cap'n Crunch's actual rank is closer to "Ensign," but nobody wants to get into it at breakfast.

Violations on Record
  • Impersonating a naval officer (ongoing, since 1963)
  • Intentional roof-of-mouth destruction — Class B misdemeanor
  • "Oops! All Berries" edition viewed as a confession; case pending
  • The ship is not real. We are choosing not to press charges on the ship.
Tier III  •  Respectable / Motivational Speaker

Frosted Flakes

"THEY'RE GRRR-EAT! (Tony is contractually obligated to say this.)"

Life Coach Adjacent Corn Flakes in a Suit Tony Has a Tiger Problem

Frosted Flakes is, at its core, a Corn Flake that made different choices. Specifically, the choice to coat itself in sugar and hire a tiger named Tony to provide around-the-clock motivational support. This is, when you think about it, either the smartest business decision in cereal history or a cry for help. Possibly both.

Tony the Tiger is not doing great. He's been enthusiastic about everything for over seventy years. Everything is GRRR-EAT. Rain? GRRR-EAT. Mondays? GRRR-EAT. Existential malaise? GRRR-EAT. His therapist (a golden retriever named Dr. Biscuit) is very concerned.

Frosted Flakes positions itself as aspirational — athletic kids, soccer goals, optimism — while being legally distinct from "health food" by a margin of about 37 grams of sugar per serving. Tony has been asked not to look at the nutrition label.

Tony's Incident Log
  • Said "GRRR-EAT" 847,000 times in 2024 alone; may be a cry for help
  • Once tried to unionize; the bowl said no
  • Holds the world record for "most aggressively positive entity in the grocery aisle"
  • Has never, in 70 years, been asked how HE is doing
Tier IV  •  Clinical Case Study

Cocoa Puffs

"I am FINE. Sonny is FINE. Everything is FINE. CUCKOO."

Self-Admitted Addict Voluntary Cuckoo Intervention Pending

Cocoa Puffs is unique among cereals in that it is the only brand that has publicly, proudly, and legally confessed to an addiction problem in its own advertising for sixty years. Sonny the Cuckoo Bird goes cuckoo for Cocoa Puffs. This is in the slogan. The company has done this on purpose.

Multiple interventions have been staged. Sonny returns to the Cocoa Puffs every single time. His family (a collection of sensible whole-grain cereals) has set boundaries. The boundaries have not worked. Sonny will not be contained. Sonny is, in a clinical sense, the most honest mascot in food advertising history, because at least he admits what he is.

On the social hierarchy, Cocoa Puffs occupies a complicated position: simultaneously sympathetic (it's honest!) and alarming (it's so honest). Grape-Nuts has written to the HOA about it. Granola wrote a lengthy thread about the "systemic pathologizing of sugar dependency." Nobody read past the first paragraph.

Intervention Notes
  • Intervention #1 (1963): Failed. Sonny ate Cocoa Puffs during the intervention.
  • Intervention #2–47: See above
  • Has attended "Going Nuts for Oats" support group; relapsed at the parking lot vending machine
  • Is somehow doing fine; this baffles medical science
Tier I  •  Landed Gentry (Grumpy Branch)

Raisin Bran

"TWO SCOOPS. I said TWO SCOOPS. Write it down."

Two Scoops (Non-Negotiable) Fiber Oligarch Both Suns Are Frowning

Raisin Bran has two suns on its box. Both suns are frowning. This is either the most honest piece of branding in American commercial history or a profound cry for help from whichever graphic designer was assigned to this account in 1926 and never recovered.

Raisin Bran does not ask for your affection. It does not offer charm, magically delicious marshmallows, or a friendly tiger. It offers TWO SCOOPS of raisins and the implicit threat that if you do not eat your fiber, consequences will follow. What consequences? Raisin Bran will not say. It doesn't need to. The two frowning suns are enough.

In the cereal hierarchy, Raisin Bran sits near the top through sheer gravitas and the implied virtue of regularity. Doctors mention it approvingly. This is Raisin Bran's entire personality. It has no others.

Property of Raisin Bran, LLC
  • Has counted the raisins in every box since 1926; results classified
  • Filed a lawsuit against a competitor for claiming 1.5 scoops (settled out of court)
  • The suns have not smiled since the Carter administration; reason unknown
  • Grape-Nuts considers Raisin Bran "new money"; Raisin Bran is livid
Tier III  •  Salt of the Earth

Honey Bunches of Oats

"Just happy to be here, honestly."

Actually Nice No Agenda Everyone's Second Favorite

Honey Bunches of Oats is the only cereal in the hierarchy without a criminal record, a trust fund, an unfinished dissertation, an addiction, or a grandiose mascot. It is simply nice. It has little honey clusters. They are crunchy. It is good with milk, good without milk, good as a snack, good as dinner when you are too tired to cook. No judgment.

In a class system defined by pretension, criminality, and old money resentment, Honey Bunches of Oats floats through with calm, cheerful indifference to the drama. Granola has tried to recruit it into the Academic Class. Lucky Charms once attempted to involve it in a scheme. Grape-Nuts has sent three invitations to the HOA. Honey Bunches of Oats has politely declined all of them and gone back to being reliably pleasant.

It is, in a word, fine. Wonderfully, beautifully, uncomplicated fine. In a world of screaming cereals, fine is underrated.

Record & Reputation
  • Zero criminal offenses; verified by three background checks
  • Has been everyone's backup cereal at least once; considers this an honor
  • Once referred to as "the Switzerland of the cereal aisle" — not offended
  • Has a good relationship with both Cheerios AND Special K, which is remarkable
Tier III  •  The Stoic Ancestor

Corn Flakes

"I was here first. All of this is my fault."

Since 1906 The Original Deep Regret

Corn Flakes invented breakfast cereal, and it has had complicated feelings about it ever since. It is 1906. John Harvey Kellogg is very confident. Corn Flakes is born into a world that does not yet have Lucky Charms, Cap'n Crunch, or Froot Loops. It could not have known. No one could have known.

Corn Flakes now watches the cereal aisle — the sugar, the mascots, the marshmallows, the fake captains — with the weary resignation of a Founding Father who has read the comments section. "I did not intend for all of this," says the rooster on the box, silently, with his eyes. "I simply wanted people to eat something in the morning."

In the hierarchy, Corn Flakes holds a position of ancestral respect. It is not flashy. It does not advertise. The rooster has been through things. The rooster has seen things. Leave the rooster alone.

Historical Record
  • Responsible, indirectly, for all of this. Has not forgiven itself.
  • Once tried to get Froot Loops expelled from the shelf; failed
  • The rooster does not have a name; this is intentional and says everything
  • Holds a grudge against Frosted Flakes: "It's just me, but worse" (accurate)
Tier II  •  Wellness Industrial Complex

Special K

"Have you heard about the Two-Week Challenge?"

Diet Culture Ambassador Very Confident About Your Body Kellogg's Corporate Favorite

Special K will not stop talking about the Two-Week Challenge. What is the Two-Week Challenge? Nobody fully knows. It is something about replacing meals with Special K. It will, allegedly, change your life. Special K is very excited about it. Special K has been excited about it since 1955. The challenge has never ended. There is no finish line. There is only Special K, forever, believing in you in a slightly unsettling way.

In the social hierarchy, Special K occupies a position of smooth corporate confidence. It went to Kellogg's Business School (this is a real place; Special K finds it extremely validating). It believes in "wellness," "intentions," and "the journey," which are words that cost nothing and yet somehow command a premium price per box.

Special K is the cereal that your company's wellness initiative recommends. This should tell you everything you need to know about both Special K and your company's wellness initiative.

Wellness File
  • Has issued 14 "Two-Week Challenges" since 1955; outcomes unverified
  • Tried to relaunch as "Special K Protein" — panel of nutritionists unimpressed
  • Refers to breakfast as "an opportunity for intentional nourishment"
  • Has never once said what makes it special; relies on the K to carry the weight

And So the Bowl Turns

The cereal aisle is, at its heart, a microcosm of human civilization: landed gentry hoarding fiber and real estate, a charismatic criminal with marshmallow charm, anxious academics with unfinished theses, and one cheerful, uncomplicated cereal who just wants everyone to get along and has good clusters.

Every morning, you open the cabinet and choose your allegiance. Choose wisely. Or don't. The frowning suns of Raisin Bran are watching. They are always watching.
